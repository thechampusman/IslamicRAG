# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the spirit of seeking beneficial knowledge (*'ilm nāfi'*) and serving the Muslim community, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project and our community a respectful, constructive, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion (within Islamic boundaries), or sexual orientation.

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all and expect our code of conduct to be honored by everyone who participates in our community formally or informally, or claims any affiliation with the project.

---

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior
Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Respectful Communication**: Using welcoming, respectful, and professional language
- **Islamic Adab**: Practicing good manners (*adab*) in all interactions
- **Constructive Feedback**: Providing and gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- **Authenticity Focus**: Prioritizing accuracy and proper attribution of Islamic sources
- **Community First**: Showing empathy toward other community members
- **Scholarly Integrity**: Respecting differences in Islamic schools of thought (*madhāhib*) and avoiding takfir (declaring someone outside the fold of Islam)
- **Knowledge Sharing**: Helping others learn and grow in their technical and Islamic understanding

### Unacceptable Behavior
Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants include:

- **Harassment**: Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- **Privacy Violation**: Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- **Disrespect**: Any conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional or community setting
- **False Information**: Deliberately spreading inauthentic or fabricated Islamic content
- **Religious Disputes**: Engaging in sectarian arguments or promoting deviant beliefs outside mainstream Islam
- **Spam**: Repeatedly posting off-topic content or promotional material
- **Undermining Project Goals**: Attempting to introduce non-Islamic content as authentic

---

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, or to ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for other behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community. Examples of representing a project or community include:

- Using an official project e-mail address
- Posting via an official social media account
- Acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event
- Contributing to the GitHub repository (issues, PRs, discussions)
- Participating in community forums or chat channels

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported privately by contacting the project maintainer:

**Usman Gour** (thechampusman)

- **Telegram**: [@thechampusman](https://t.me/thechampusman) (preferred for quick response)
- **GitHub**: [@thechampusman](https://github.com/thechampusman) (private message or email)

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The project team is obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter of an incident.

### Response Timeline

- **Acknowledgment**: Within 48 hours of receiving a report
- **Investigation**: Within 7 days, maintainer will gather information from all parties
- **Decision**: A resolution will be communicated within 14 days
- **Appeal**: If you disagree with a decision, you may request a review within 7 days

### Enforcement Actions

Project maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other members of the project's leadership.

Depending on the severity and frequency of violations, actions may include:

1. **Warning**: A private, written warning providing clarity around the nature of the violation and explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate
2. **Temporary Ban**: A temporary ban from all project interactions (issues, PRs, discussions) for a specified period
3. **Permanent Ban**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the project community

All enforcement decisions will be made fairly, transparently, and with respect for the reporter's confidentiality.

## Attribution & Islamic Principles

This Code of Conduct is adapted from the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org), version 2.1, and incorporates principles from Islamic ethics (*akhlāq*) emphasizing:

- **Adab** (good manners and respect in knowledge-seeking)
- **Amānah** (trustworthiness in conveying Islamic knowledge)
- **Ikhlās** (sincerity in contributing for the sake of Allah)
- **Ihsān** (excellence and doing good)

We recognize that this project serves a specific purpose: providing Muslims with authentic Islamic knowledge through technology. As such, we maintain Islamic values in our community interactions while welcoming technical contributions from anyone who respects our mission.
